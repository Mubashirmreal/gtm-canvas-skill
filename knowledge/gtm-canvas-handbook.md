## Disclaimer

GTM Canvas is **not a holy grail**. It's observations from 2000+ ideas and GTM methods, condensed into a structured framework. Pick what works, leave the rest. It's like **Google Maps**: you set the destination, it gives the route — you drive.

**Who it's for:** Founders, PMs, marketers launching products or features. Works for B2B / B2C / B2G, tech / non-tech, hardware / software.

## Step 1: Assign GTM Lead

The GTM Lead coordinates:
- GTMC Sprints (2 weeks each)
- Cross-team coordination (product / marketing / content)
- Progress tracking
- Leadership buy-in
- Product iterations based on GTM data

**Iterations:** Marketing is iterative. End each 2-week sprint with:
1. Dated GTM Canvas + iteration number
2. Campaign objective
3. Team assignments
4. 2-week review: progress, challenges, results

## GTM Canvas Components

### 1. Primary Feature

**THE feature your product is known for.** Users come for this; they would be devastated if it were removed.

**Example:** Google Search (not Google Ads).

---

### 2. Primary Sellable Feature

**What customers pay for.** May differ from the primary feature.

**Example:** Google Ads.

---

### 3. Differentiator

**Main difference from competitors.** Wins the comparison war — branding, pricing, quality, reviews, UX.

---

### 4. Alpha (Unfair Advantage)

**Hardest one:** What can competitors **never replicate**?

**Examples:**
- Premium domain with built-in traffic
- Exclusive relationships (celebrity / influencer)
- Proprietary technology or IP
- Exclusive access (data, raw materials)

---

### 5. Handpick Users (Atomic Network)

**GTM is linear at first:** 1 → 10 → 100 → 1K → 10K → ...

Handpick **100 sticky users** from your **1st-degree network**:
- Phone contacts
- WhatsApp / Email lists
- LinkedIn / Instagram / Twitter connections
- Offline: school / office / neighbors / events / community groups

**Process:**
1. Personal outreach ("Hey [name], we met at...")
2. Manual onboarding
3. Track: engagement, retention, **time to wow**
4. Identify **influencers** — people who will promote without being asked

**Why:** Low-quality first users lead to a dead end.

---

### 6. Profiling (Who)

Profile your **best potential users** (2–5 profiles maximum):
- Demographics
- Role / job
- Goals
- Pain points
- Where they spend their time

**Fewer profiles = better** — diverse users require diverse content strategies, which dilutes effort.

---

### 7. Source (Where)

**Where do profiled users live?** Where do they spend the most time? Where is their best attention?

**Online:** Specific subreddits, LinkedIn groups, YouTube channels, newsletters, Discord / Slack communities.  
**Offline:** Events, meetups, conferences.

---

### 8. Medium/Channel (How)

Reverse-engineer channels from the "Source" answers. Four quadrants:

#### Inbound Organic (pull, free)
SEO, content marketing, social media, YouTube, Reddit / Quora, LinkedIn, Product Led Growth (PLG), communities / partnerships.

#### Inbound Paid (pull, paid)
Google Ads, Meta / Facebook / Instagram Ads, influencer campaigns, directory submissions, banner promotions.

#### Outbound Organic (push, free)
Email outreach, LinkedIn connections, events & meetups, webinars, guest podcasting, PR, Discord / communities, networking.

#### Outbound Paid (push, paid)
TV / newspaper / radio ads, banner advertising, event sponsorships, giveaways, coupon codes, endorsements, product tech reviews.

#### Partnerships (Escape Velocity)
Brands, communities, platforms, products / services, organizations (corporate / NGO / government), campaigns, events / expos / conferences, distributors.

---

### Distribute vs. Discover

- **Distribute:** Visibility and consideration — portals, Facebook groups, directories.
- **Discover:** Intent capture — search engines, classifieds.

---

### 9. Results (Sprint Execution)

**Each sprint (2 weeks):**
1. Launch campaigns from your canvas
2. Measure and optimize
3. Kill underperformers / create new experiments based on data
4. Repeat with an updated canvas

> **Note:** No Product Hunt or PLG factors on Day 0 — they do not work until you have meaningful traction.
